BRepTopology¶

A BRepTopology is a data object describing the incidence relationships (topology) of four types of elements in a two dimensional manifold: regions (faces), loops, edges, and vertices. The fundamental element describing the connectivity is the edge. An edge has a startpoint and an endpoint that determine the orientation of the edge. Edges meet at their str- or endpoints, these positions are called vertices. An edge is incident to two regions, one to its left side another to its right side. The BRepTopology functions frequently refer to edges through their oriented left- or right sided halfedges. These side - and orientation flags are useful when traversing the topology graph.
